Ingredients

  • 2 cups plain Greek yogurt
  • Juice from half a lemon
  • 4 tsp each of cumin, cayenne, black pepper, cinnamon, and paprika (can be substituted with equal total amount of a garam masala. 16 tsp with 4 additional tsp black pepper)
  • 2 tbsp fresh minced ginger
  • 8 tsp salt
  • 5 Chicken breasts cut into 1”-2” cubes
  • 1 tbsp butter/Ghee
  • 1 whole jalapeno, finely chopped
  • 3-5 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ yellow onion, finely chopped
  • 28 oz can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 pint heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup cilantro, as garnish

Steps

  1. Greek yogurt into a mixing bowl, add 1 tablespoon lemon juice, half of spices/salt, ginger, and mix. Sear chicken in batches in a large Dutch oven, avoiding overcrowding, to brown outside but not cook through. Some oil can be used if sticking is a problem, but remaining bits in pan are fine/desirable. Add seared chicken to yogurt mixture, making sure pieces are coated to marinade as you continue batch cooking. Set aside.
  2. In same Dutch oven used to cook chicken, 1 tablespoon of butter to a large pot on medium heat. Add jalapeno, garlic, onion and sauté until soft/translucent. Add remaining spices not added to yogurt mixture, and stir well to coat contents of pot, ~30 sec. Add crushed tomatoes, and slowly pour in heavy cream. Heat 5 minutes, stirring frequently until uniform color, and add the bowl of chicken and Greek yogurt mixture. Sit on heat until the chicken is completely cooked through and the sauce reduces to a nice medium orange color and thickness.
  3. Serve with rice on side, cilantro on top.
