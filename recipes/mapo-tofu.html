Ingredients

  • 1 package silken tofu
  • ½ pound ground pork
  • 2 tsp chili oil
  • 2 tbsp fermented black beans:
  • 2 1/2 tbsp Dou Ban Jiang
  • 1 tsp Sichuan peppercorn oil (as below)
  • 1 tsp (crushed) Sichuan peppercorn
  • 1 tbsp red pepper powder/chili powder
  • 1 1/2 tsp ginger
  • 5 cloves garlic
  • 3 sprigs spring/green onion
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 cup chicken/vegetable stock or water
  • 1 tsp salt for boiling water
  • 1 tsp potato/corn starch
  • 1 tsp water

Steps

  1. Boil saltwater in large pot in preparation for tofu. Ideally, also start rice as dish cooks quickly. Make Sichuan powder by briefly roasting and crushing Sichuan peppercorns. In a small skillet, cook another 2 tbsp of peppercorn in ¼ cp of a neutral oil to make Sichuan peppercorn oil (1-2 min, remove peppercorns).
  2. Mince garlic and ginger. Slice the white parts of 3 sprigs of green onion, and around 2 of the green parts. Reserve green for garnish at end. Finely chop fermented black beans and doubanjiang. Make slurry with 1 tsp each of potato starch and water. Finally, when saltwater is at a boil, cut drained tofu into blocks and boil until begins to float.
  3. Heat wok until hot. Add 2 tsp vegetable oil and cook pork until color change. Push pork to side of wok and add aromatics (white part of green onion, ginger, garlic), cook on low heat until fragrant, about 15 seconds. Returning to higher heat, add back meat, stir, and add fermented black beans. Continue for 30 seconds till beans are fragrant and add doubanjiang. Stir to combine. Add chicken stock, chili powder, chili oil, and peppercorn oil. Stir, add boiled tofu. Cook 2-3 minutes, pushing tofu (rather than stirring vigorously possibly breaking the tofu) and pouring sauce over the blocks of tofu. Add sugar and soy sauce. Add slurry. Add sesame oil.
  4. Plate with crushed peppercorn and reserved green parts of green onion.
